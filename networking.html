How to Build a Strong Network

Step 1: Find Alumni

  • UMSI LinkedIn Group: Join here
  • UMSI Alumni Tool: Explore alumni
  • CareerLink (employer contacts & events): Login
  • UCAN: Connect here
  • Your own network — friends, peers, mentors, faculty

Step 2: Get a Response

Reach out to them in a way that will get you a response. Average response rate to “cold messages” is around 20%. Each contact you connect with is valuable.

Do’s

  • Keep your message short
  • Establish a clear time frame (i.e. 20 minutes in the next 2 weeks)
  • Personalize your message

Don’ts

  • Don’t ask for info easily available online
  • Never send your resume first. Ask permission
  • Don’t begin by asking for a referral or a job

Email Template Example

Subject: UMSI Student Seeking Career Advice

Dear Mr. Black,

I am a first year UMSI student pursuing an MSI with a concentration in XXX.
I am exploring a career in XXX at XXX and came across your LinkedIn profile.
I’d love to speak with you for 20-30 minutes about your career path and
steps you recommend for someone considering a similar career.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Sincerely,
Patti Piper MSI '18

The LAMP Framework

Use the LAMP method to guide your networking conversations:

  • L — Listen: Pay attention to the other person’s experiences and insights. Take notes and show genuine interest.
  • A — Ask: Prepare thoughtful questions about their career path, organization, or industry.
  • M — Motivate: Share your enthusiasm and goals so they understand why you’re reaching out.
  • P — Plan: Close by suggesting a follow-up or asking if you can stay in touch. Send a thank-you message afterwards.

The LAMP framework makes networking conversations structured, respectful, and memorable.
